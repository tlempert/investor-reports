4. THE YELLOW PAGES TEST — MOAT DECAY PATTERN

This is where I must be brutally honest.

Comparable monopolies that appeared invincible: - IBM Mainframe (1970s-80s): Hardware lock-in seemed unbreakable until the PC changed the unit economics of compute. - Intel x86 (1990s-2010s): Architecture monopoly with ecosystem lock-in. The NVIDIA parallel I find most instructive is Intel x86. Intel had a comparable software ecosystem moat — the Wintel platform — and it took 20+ years for ARM to meaningfully erode it. The erosion happened not through direct competition but through a paradigm shift in the dominant workload (mobile, not desktop).

What could cause NVIDIA's equivalent paradigm shift?

  1. Inference economics vs. training economics: Current hyperscaler spend is dominated by training. If inference at scale migrates to specialized silicon optimized for specific model architectures, CUDA's generality becomes overhead, not advantage.

  3. Model efficiency compressing demand: If algorithmic improvements (like we've seen with efficiency gains in frontier models) reduce compute requirements faster than new use cases consume it, the insatiable demand narrative breaks.

The moat is built on something largely permanent (switching costs + ecosystem), but the rate of value capture depends on workload growth continuing to exceed efficiency gains. That is a real risk, not a theoretical one.

1. THE "EBITDA" CHARADE — Quality of Earnings

SBC: The Hidden Dilution Tax

The dossier books SBC at $6.39B in FY2026, which looks manageable at 3.0% of revenue. Management will crow about this declining percentage. That's the trick.

Burry's real claim is the right one: actual economic dilution cost was $112.5B using market price of shares granted, not $6.4B booked. The GAAP books record SBC at grant-date fair value. When the stock trades at multiples of that value, the true cost to shareholders is the replacement cost — what you'd pay today to buy those shares in the open market. The $6.4B "cost" is accounting fiction.

Starting FY2027, NVIDIA will include SBC in non-GAAP measures. This is the tell.
